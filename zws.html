Executive Summary

Q1 2026 marked a decisive inflection for ZWS — organic growth of 11%, 580 bps above consensus, paired with 35% EPS growth and EBITDA margin expansion of 160 bps. The underlying drivers are structural: lead remediation mandates, a recurring filtration revenue model at 60–70% gross margins, and a de-risked supply chain that competitors cannot quickly replicate.

01

Triple consensus beat in Q1 2026. Revenue $433.0M (+3.3% vs. estimate), diluted EPS $0.35 (+16.7%), organic growth 580 bps above the +5.2% expected — the largest single-quarter organic beat in recent company history.

02

Lead remediation mandate — peak volume 2026–2027. The federal LCRR lead pipe replacement rule is driving a multi-year institutional replacement cycle. ZWS holds an estimated 12–15% share of the institutional water market, positioning it as the primary beneficiary.

03

Filtration: a recurring revenue engine at scale. Filtration represents >40% of revenue at 60–70% gross margins — cartridge replacement revenue creates durable customer stickiness and mix-shift toward higher-margin annuity-type streams.

04

Supply chain de-risking creates a structural tariff moat. China COGS declining to <3% of total cost base by end-2026, through scaling in Mexico and Southeast Asia — a meaningful tariff advantage vs. peers still reliant on Chinese manufacturing.

02

Q1 2026 Results

Triple Consensus Beat

Zurn Elkay reported Q1 2026 revenue of $433.0M, +3.3% above consensus, with organic growth of +11.0% — 580 basis points ahead of the +5.2% estimate. EBITDA of $116.0M delivered a 26.8% margin, expanding 160 bps year-on-year. Diluted EPS of $0.35 exceeded the $0.30 consensus estimate by 16.7%, driven by operating leverage and favorable product mix.

Segment Performance

The Institutional segment (49.4% of Q1 revenue, ~$213.9M) is the primary growth engine, directly benefiting from the LCRR lead pipe replacement mandate accelerating K–12, healthcare, and higher-education upgrades. Commercial (29.2%, ~$126.4M) remained resilient. Industrial & Municipal (21.3%, ~$92.2M) is an underappreciated growth avenue as municipal water systems undertake infrastructure modernization.

Growth Outlook

Revenue Path to $1.3B+

We project revenue growing from $1,145M (2025A) to $1,335M by 2029E, a 3.9% CAGR. Headline growth decelerates from 6.5% in 2025A as the lead remediation hardware cycle matures — but this masks a favorable mix shift: filtration recurring revenue is expected to approach 45–50% of total revenue by 2027, improving earnings quality and visibility.

Margin Profile and EPS Trajectory

EBITDA margins face modest compression from 20.7% (2025A) to 18.1% (2029E) as high-margin remediation hardware volumes normalize. However, filtration cartridge mix expansion — at 60–70% gross margins — partially offsets this. EPS peaks at $1.32 in 2027E before modest compression driven by volume normalization and mix effects. The $100M+ buyback program provides meaningful EPS support over the outlook period.

Risk  −34.8%
Valuation — Premium Justified by Quality, Not by Margin

At 40.98x forward P/E, ZWS trades at a 90% premium to the peer median of 21.5x. Our DCF (7.8% WACC, 2.5% terminal growth) yields a fair value of $52.50; applying trading comps narrows this to $41.40. The blended intrinsic value of $40.95 represents a 28.5% discount to current prices. We accept this premium because filtration margin quality, institutional stickiness, and supply chain de-risking are not fully captured in peer multiple analysis. Our $62 target reflects a bull-case scenario with 20% probability weighting.

Peer Valuation Context

ZWS trades at a significant premium to Watts Water (~22x), Fortune Brands (~18x), and the water infrastructure peer median of 21.5x. Badger Meter (~30x), the closest pure-play comps, provides some justification for a premium multiple. We believe ZWS deserves a 30–35x P/E in a bull execution scenario, driven by filtration mix and recurring revenue quality — but acknowledging current price embeds this optimism.

Headwinds vs. Catalysts

The risk/reward for 2026 weighs valuation compression as the primary downside risk — the 28.5% premium to intrinsic value could unwind sharply if organic growth disappoints. Against this, infrastructure spending acceleration and continued filtration mix improvement are the key upside levers. Q2 2026 results (expected +8–9% core growth) will be the near-term test of the bull thesis.

05

Conclusion

A quality water franchise with structural tailwinds — the premium multiple is the price of admission.

Zurn Elkay is a genuinely differentiated water infrastructure platform: institutional market leadership, a recurring filtration revenue engine at exceptional margins, and a supply chain increasingly insulated from tariff risk. Q1 2026 validated the organic growth trajectory beyond what consensus expected. The risk is not the business — it is the entry price.

We initiate at Overweight with a price target of $62 (+15.5% upside), reflecting our bull case of infrastructure spending acceleration and filtration mix expansion. The primary risk is a valuation re-rating should organic growth moderate toward the 3–4% range. Q2 2026 results (expected 8–9% core growth) are the near-term catalyst: a second consecutive double-digit organic beat would significantly de-risk the investment case. We recommend building positions on weakness toward $48–50.
