Executive Summary

Q2 2026 significantly beats consensus ($111.2B in revenue, +17.0% year-on-year vs. +15.5% expected), with a spectacularly accelerating iPhone division (+56.7%) driving momentum. Services reached an all-time high. Q3 2026 guidance is set at +14–17%.

01

Strong revenue and EPS beat. Revenue of $111.2B (+1.5% vs. consensus), diluted EPS of $2.10 beating estimates of $1.92 by +9.4% — accelerating operating leverage.

02

Services at an all-time high. $30.98B (+15.5% year-on-year), now representing 28% of revenue — a level never previously achieved.

03

iPhone sharply accelerating. $56.99B (+56.7% year-on-year), representing 51.3% of total revenue, supported by strong upgrade demand.

04

Margin expansion and shareholder returns. Gross margin at 49.3% (+110 bps year-on-year), new $100B buyback program and dividend raised 4%.

02

Results Analysis

Consensus Beat

Apple reported revenue of $111.2B in Q2 2026, beating consensus by $1.54B (+1.5%), representing growth of +17.0% year-on-year. Diluted EPS of $2.10 exceeded estimates of $1.92 by +9.4%, benefiting from operating leverage and favorable tax conditions. The acceleration is structural: the revenue beat has been widening quarter after quarter.

The EPS acceleration reflects growing operating leverage: +4.2% beat in Q4 2025, then +6.8% in Q1 2026, and +9.4% in Q2 2026 — a strong signal of continued margin expansion beyond expectations.

Segment and Geographic Performance

iPhone delivered spectacular growth of +56.7% to $56.99B, now representing 51.3% of total revenue. Services reached an all-time high of $30.98B (+15.5% year-on-year). Geographically, Greater China delivered $20.5B (+28.1% year-on-year), demonstrating remarkable resilience. The Americas remain the largest market at approximately $50.0B.

2026 Outlook

Q3 2026 Guidance

Apple guided for revenue growth of +14 to +17% year-on-year in Q3 2026 (base: $97.4B in Q3 2025), implying approximately $112B at the midpoint. This trajectory confirms robust demand despite modest seasonal deceleration.

The iPhone 16 launch cycle in September 2026 is the primary near-term catalyst. With an installed base of 900 million devices — 15–20% of which have not upgraded in over 3 years — the upgrade cycle could exceed prior cycles, further supported by new on-device AI features.

Services now represent 28% of total revenue, up 15.5% year-on-year to $30.98B — an absolute record. We estimate Services could reach 32–35% of revenue by FY2028, lifting the consolidated gross margin toward 51–52%.

05

Conclusion

A marked acceleration, underpinned by robust fundamentals and multiple growth catalysts.

Apple demonstrates a rare capacity for acceleration at this scale. The consensus beat, iPhone leadership, and Services at a record high support a positive medium-term outlook. Gross margin at 49.3% illustrates pricing power and a favorable mix shift toward higher value-added activities.

The valuation at 30.5x forward P/E remains attractive given Apple's growth profile (EPS +22% year-on-year). We upgrade our recommendation to Overweight with a price target of $290, implying upside of +17.4% from current levels. The iPhone 16 launch cycle in September 2026 is the primary near-term catalyst, followed by continued Services acceleration toward 30–35% of revenue.
